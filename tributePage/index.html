Professor Chinua Achebe

The Father of african literature.

Here is a timeline of Professor Chinua Achebe's life:

  • 1930- was born in the village of ogidi, southeastern Nigeria

  • 1936 - Primary education at st Philip's primary school.

  • 1948 - Started writing while he was a student at the university of ibadan After reading Joyce Cary's 1939 work "Mister Johnson" about a cheerful Nigerian man who (among other things) works for an abusive British storeowner, he was so disturbed by the book's portrayal of its Nigerian characters as either savages or buffoons that he decided to become a writer.

  • 1958 - One of his most famous book "Things fall apart" was published.

  • 1961 - Got married to a woman he met at NBS Enugu, Nigeria whose name is Christiana Chinwe (Christie) Okoli

  • 2002 - The book (Things fall apart), in recognition of its universality, appears in the Bokklubben World Library collection "proposed by one hundred writers from fifty-four different countries, compiled and organized by the Norwegian Book Club.

  • 2018 - The book is included in the Encyclopedia Britannica's list of "12 Novels Considered the 'Greatest Book Ever Written'".

  • 2013 - Dies at the age of 83.
